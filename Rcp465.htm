Aubergine Roulades filled with Beef Mince

Ingredients for 4 Persons:
1.5 kg large aubergines
3 tablespoons Olive oil
500 g Beef mince
1 medium Onion
1/2 cup fresh white Breadcrumbs
2 tablespoons fresh Mint
Salt, black pepper
2 Garlic Cloves
3 tins peeled and diced Tomatoes
2 small red hot Chilies
1 green Pepper
1/2 cup crubled Feta

Preparation:

Preparation time: 20 minutes, Cooking time: 45 minutes

Chop onion finely. Chop mint finely.

Mix onion, beef mince, breadcrumbs, mint, salt and pepper in a mixing bowl for the stuffing.

Preheat oven to 200°C.

Slice the aubergine lengthways in narrow strips and shallow fry until soft.

Remove and place on absorbent paper.

Chop garlic finely. Chop chilies and green pepper.

Heat the remaining olive oil in the frying pan and add garlic, tomatoes, chilies and pepper and cook for roughly 15 minutes until reduced to a sauce consistency. Season.

Spread a little of the tomato sauce over the bottom of a ceramic roaster. Stuff the aubergine slices with approximately 1.5 tablespoons of the filling and roll them up. Continue until you have used up all the aubergine and stuffing.

Place the aubergine rolls, flap side down on top of the tomato sauce.

Pour over the remaining tomato sauche, crumble over Feta and bake for about 45 minutes until thoroughly cooked.

Source:

Recipe Booklet from Checkers, Tastes of the World 2005

Valuation:

Tasted fantastic on 11 Feb 2007. The Feta doesn't melt but gives a nice crunchy bite to the dish. RE

Made the dish with 1 large eggplant and had plenty for 2 people. I skipped the mint in honour of the contiental European palette. We enjoyed the spiciness of the hot Thai chilli I used which delicately balanced the many flavours combined in this dish. Sandra suggests it could be served with a starch side-dish (noodles, potatoes, rice) but I prefer to have one more roll of mincemeat eggplant instead. RE 2.5.2009
