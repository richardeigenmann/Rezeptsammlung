Ossobuco

Ingredients 4 Portions:
4 pieces veal shank (4-5 cm thick)
Salt, Pepper
2 tbs flour
2 onions
1 large or 5 small carrot(s)
2 cloves garlic
2 springs celery
2 springs thyme
3 beef tomatoes
25 g butter
3 tbs olive oil
1 piece lemon rind
3 dl dry whitewine
3 dl veal stock
bayleaf
2 springs rosemary or marjoram

Mise en Place:

Snip the outer edge of the shank slices with scissors or a sharp knife to prevent them from curling upon cooking

Sprinkle them with salt and pepper and coat with flour

Peel and finely chop the onions. Peel the carrot and cut it into small pieces. Finely chop the peeled garlic cloves, the celery and the thyme. But not the rosemary.

Cut a cross in the tomato bases and place in hot water for a few moments.

Skin the tomatoes and remove the seeds and cut the flesh into cubes.

Cook:

Heat the butter with the olive oil and brown meat briefly.

Add the onions, carrot, garlic, celery, tomatos, thyme and lemon rind and braise for a few minutes.

Add the wine and veal stock and braise gently, with some bayleaf, with the lid on the pan for 1 hour and 30 minutes (or 20 minutes in the pressure cooker). The meat should not fall off the bone. Add a few tablespoons of veal stock if the sauce appears too thick.

If the sauce appears too thin, thicken it with 15 grams of butter mixed with 1/2 tablespoon of flour.

The braising juices can be purified with a hand mixer if required.

Chop the sprigs of rosemary or marjoram and sprinkle these over the dish.

Tips:

Serve the ossobuco with a risotto with grated parmesan cheese or pecorino

Add a special touch to the dish by adding so-called gremolata at the last moment. Gremolata is a mixture of freshly chopped parsley, grated lemon rind, freshly chopped garlic and ground anchovy fillets or sardines.

Add 2 tablespoons of fresh orange juice to the sauce for a fresher taste.

Source:

Pamphlet of the ESA
