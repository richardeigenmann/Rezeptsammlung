Roast Pork Provencale

Ingredients:
3 cloves garlic, slivered
1 kg pork roast, rolled boned
2/3 cup white wine, dry
2 lemons, juice of
1/4 cup olive oil
3 medium onions
or 8 green onions with tops
2 bay leaves
1 EL dried thyme
1 TL salt
1/2 TL pepper
Watercress (optional)

Preparataion:

Marinate: Insert garlic under fat of meat. Place meat in doubled plastic bags. Stir together wine, lemon, juice, oil, chopped onions, bay leaves, thyme, salt and pepper; pour over meat. Tie bags securely, place in dish and refrigerate 24 hours, turning occasionally.

Remove meat from marinade and place fat side up on rack in shallow pan. Let stand at room temperature for 1 hour.

Strain marinade and reserve. Toast in preheated 180 °C oven about 80 minutes, basting with marinade twice during roasting. (Use a roast thermometer and check the core temperature). Transfer roast to carving board and let stand while preparing sauce.

Add reserved marinade to roasting pan, cook and stir to loosen pan drippings; pour into warm gravy boat.

Slice meat thin, garnish with watercress and serve with sauce.

Source:

Herbert Eigenmann, Code: SWP

Valuation:

Since I was only cooking for two I got a tiny litte rolled roast. This did not produce a lot of sauce. So I reduced the marinate that tasted strongly of lemon and added a good measure of port wine. (Obviously not the vintage stuff; I got the Sandeman from the top shelf of my local discounter.) This turned out absolutely divine! Sandtra took the picture. RE 31.12.2009
